MAIZE

Maize, also known as corn (North American and Australian English), is a cereal grain first domesticated by indigenous peoples in southern Mexico about 10,000 years ago.[1][2] The leafy stalk of the plant produces pollen inflorescences (or "silk") and separate ovuliferous inflorescences called ears that when fertilized yield kernels or seeds, which are fruits.
